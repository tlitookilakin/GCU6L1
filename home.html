Drawing

In the last two years I've picked up art as a hobby and it's been a lot of fun. Mostly I draw characters of my own creation- many of them my player characters for tabletop rpgs that I play with my friends. I tend to favor animals over humans, in particular dragons, ponies, cats, and wolves. I usually sketch them with paper, pencil, and ink, then trace and color them digitally. In the past I've also done fully-digital vector-based artwork.

Modding Games

I've been modding games for a while, but I've only been doing c# modding for about 3 years. I mod Stardew Valley almost exclusively, usually to make things that I want to use myself. It's a lot of fun to tinker with Harmony, and transpilers in particular make me feel like a wizard. The mod I am most proud of is probably Happy Home Designer.

Favorite Books

The Locked Tomb

The Locked Tomb is actually a series- originally, it was meant to be a trilogy, but is now on its fourth book. It's an alternate future sci-fi setting, where the protagonists and antagonists are both necromancers, ruled by a man who has assumed the mantle of God. It's a mishmash of several different genres, the only really good person has been dead a thousand years, and the protagonists are in the World's Worst Lesbian Situationship.

Through the Well of Pirenne

This one's fan fiction, but it's good enough that I bought a print copy. It's an incredible adventure, with lots of fresh ideas and a mixture of several different flavors of mysticism and mythology. A disillusioned teen enters a magical land she thought she'd only invented in a desperate bid to find and rescue her kidnapped kid sister before she's changed forever.

Middlegame

Despite being a relatively short standalone novel, Middlegame has so many thematic layers to it that I could write an essay unraveling them. It's a story about alchemy, familial conflict, growing up, and stories themselves. It's rich, complex, and non-linear, with a breathtaking finale.
